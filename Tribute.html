My Tribute To My Role Model Elon Musk
Early stage Education Career Projects Personal life Wealth

Elon Musk is a South African-born American businessman, best known as the CEO of Tesla, SpaceX, and owner of X (formerly Twitter). He also founded or co-founded companies like Neuralink, The Boring Company, OpenAI, and xAI.
He became wealthy after founding Zip2 and PayPal, then moved on to revolutionize space travel with reusable rockets (SpaceX) and electric vehicles (Tesla). In 2022, he acquired Twitter and rebranded it as X.
Musk has become a polarizing political figure. He supported far-right causes, was the largest donor in the 2024 U.S. election, and briefly served as a senior advisor to President Trump in 2025 before starting his own political party. His public comments and Twitter policies have drawn criticism for promoting misinformation, conspiracy theories, and hate speech.
As of May 2025, Forbes estimates his net worth at $424.7 billion, making him the world’s richest person.

Early Life

Elon Musk was born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa, to a wealthy family. His mother, Maye Musk, is a Canadian-born model and dietitian; his father, Errol Musk, is a South African engineer and entrepreneur. Elon developed an early interest in reading, computers, and programming, creating and selling his first video game at age 12.
He faced bullying and family struggles during childhood and later became estranged from his father. After finishing high school, he moved to Canada in 1989 to avoid mandatory military service in apartheid South Africa.

Education

  1. Studied briefly at the University of Pretoria.

  2. Attended Queen's University in Canada, then transferred to the University of Pennsylvania, earning degrees in Physics and Economics (1997).

  3. Dropped out of a Stanford PhD program to pursue internet ventures.

Career

  • Zip2 (1995)
    • Co-founded with his brother Kimbal. Sold to Compaq for $307 million in 1999. Musk earned $22 million.
  • X.com / PayPal (1999–2002)
    • Founded X.com, which merged with Confinity to become PayPal, later sold to eBay for $1.5 billion. Musk received $175.8 million.
  • SpaceX (2002–Present)
    • Founded with $100 million of his fortune. Developed reusable rockets (Falcon 1, 9, Heavy) Launched Starlink for global internet Won major NASA contracts Developing Starship for Mars missions Crewed flights started in 2020 Operates over 7,600 satellites as of 2025
  • Tesla (Joined 2004, CEO from 2008)
    • Became CEO and turned it into a global leader in electric vehicles (EVs) with models like Model S, 3, X, Y, and Cybertruck. Built Gigafactories Acquired SolarCity, forming Tesla Energy
  • Neuralink (2016–Present)
    • Developing brain-computer interfaces to treat neural disorders and enhance human-AI interaction. Approved for human trials in 2023 Faces ethical concerns over animal testing
  • The Boring Company (2017–Present)
    • Aims to reduce traffic with underground tunnels. Built tunnels in Las Vegas; some other projects canceled.
  • Twitter / X Corp (2022–Present)
    • Acquired Twitter for $44 billion, renamed it X. Made controversial changes (mass layoffs, blue check subscriptions) Criticized for increased hate speech and misinformation Stepped down as CEO in 2023 but remains CTO and executive chairman.
  • xAI (2023–Present)
    • Founded as a rival to OpenAI, aiming to create safe, transparent artificial general intelligence (AGI).
